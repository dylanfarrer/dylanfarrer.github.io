Research Interests

I am passionate about tackling real-world challenges in computing, with a particular focus on the design and optimization of distributed and scalable systems. My work lies at the intersection of systems architecture, networking, and storage.

Currently, I am conducting research in software-defined storage at Imperial College London under the supervision of Dr. Marios Kogias. Initially, my research investigates performance bottlenecks in datacentre-scale networked storage pipelines - specifically, the inefficiencies introduced by complex configuration parameters in software-defined storage systems.

The goal of my work is to explore intelligent, adaptive techniques that mitigate these performance issues, making modern storage architectures more robust, predictable, and scalable.

Broadly, my interests span distributed systems, storage architectures, and systems performance engineering. I am driven by the challenge of building infrastructure that is both high-performance and practically deployable at scale.
